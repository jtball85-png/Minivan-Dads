3. **ITU (intent-to-use) trademark mechanics and cost, now quantified for counsel's brief:**
   - The base application fee for each class of goods or services is $350, which as a hat-first, apparel (Class 25) brand would likely mean one class to start.
   - If already using the trademark in interstate commerce, the use-in-commerce basis applies; if not yet in use but there is a bona fide intention to use it, the intent-to-use basis applies — this includes additional filings and fees at specific times, including an allegation of use before the application can be approved.
   - Concretely: Both the Statement of Use (SOU) and Amendment to Allege Use (AAU) fees are $150 per class as of January 2025, and a filing fee of $125 per class must be paid with each extension request, which may be filed every six months for up to a total of five extensions. The mark must be put in use and an SOU filed within three years of the Notice of Allowance issuance date.
   - Bottom line for the brief: ITU is a real "hold your place in line" option (file now, defer proof-of-use), but it's not meaningfully cheaper than use-based filing at the outset ($350/class either way) — the cost/complexity shows up later, in SOU + potential extension fees, and it stretches the process out up to three years if the brand delays going to market under the mark. Trademark attorneys recommend filing an intent-to-use application as soon as the intended brand is known, since it "holds your place in line" while the brand ramps up.
   - Note: I cannot determine from public fee pages whether ITU is the *right* call for our specific timing (we may already have use-in-commerce evidence via existing sales) — that judgment call needs outside counsel or the CEO, not this desk.
