## Print presentation concepts (ranked)

**A. Titling convention — ranked:**
1. **Place + subject + sequence** ("Anacapa Kelp Study No. 2," "Rincon Swell, Study 4") — ties every piece back to the Channel Islands/Ventura coastline the CEO's whole practice is built on (diving, sailing, bodysurfing). Reads as gallery-serious without artspeak. **Top pick.**
2. **Season/year-tagged naming** (the existing "Lobster Season 2023" pattern) — already proven and low-risk, best suited to linocuts tied to a real seasonal subject (lobster, migratory birds, king tides). Keep this convention alive for subject matter that's genuinely seasonal; don't force it onto cyanotypes that aren't.
3. **Plain numbered-only titles** ("Cyanotype No. 14") — safe but flat, loses the coastal specificity that's the actual brand asset. Use only as a fallback for abstract/experimental photograms where no place-name applies.

**B. Edition vs. open strategy — ranked by medium, since one policy doesn't fit all three:**
1. **Cyanotypes & photograms: sell as true one-of-ones, never "edition of X."** Because each is UV-exposed by hand on a given day's sun and paper batch, no two are identical — calling them "1/1" isn't a marketing trick, it's accurate, and it's the strongest possible originals story for pillar 1.
2. **Linocuts: small numbered editions (e.g., 25–50), season- or theme-bound**, following the existing Lobster Season precedent — hand-pulled relief printing genuinely supports small runs, and natural scarcity ("printed only during lobster season") is a real, defensible hook rather than artificial urgency.
3. **Giclée prints: open edition, sold by size (8×10/11×14/16×20)** — per the charter these are explicitly the "accessible alternative to an original," so treating them as scarce would contradict their own positioning. Open-and-always-available is correct here.

**C. Certificate-of-authenticity wording (draft, cyanotype/photogram one-of-ones):**
> *This is an original cyanotype, hand-coated and sun-exposed by Josh Ball in Ventura, California — no negative, no edition, no repeat. [Paper: e.g. Stonehenge / Coventry Rag / Arches Hot Press]. Signed and dated on verso. One of one.*

For numbered linocuts, a simpler line/plate + number/total format ("Plate hand-carved and printed on rice paper, [X] of [Y], [season/year]") tracks the existing product copy style rather than inventing new conventions.

**D. Packaging insert (draft, applies across originals/linocuts):**
> *Handle by the edges — this piece was made the old way, with sunlight/ink and time, not a machine. [Care line specific to medium]. Questions or want to see how it was made? [blog/journal link].*

This deliberately echoes the "beyond the constraints of social media" brand line by pointing back to the journal rather than a social handle.

## Workshop one-pagers (drafts)

**Popup one-pager (cyanotype + suminagashi, in-store/brewery):**
- **What it is:** A 60–90 min hands-on popup — guests make their own cyanotype photogram AND try suminagashi ink-marbling on the same visit, leave with 2–3 pieces.
- **What's included:** All materials, paper, aprons; a take-home care card explaining how the blue keeps developing over the following days.
- **What to bring:** Nothing required; optional — a small found object (shell, leaf, key) for the photogram.
- **Price framing (draft only, not a final number — CEO's call):** Position as "a souvenir you make, not buy" — anchor against a framed print price point rather than a class price point, since guests leave with physical art, not just an experience.

**Outdoor forage-and-print one-pager (hike → photogram → beach/park printing):**
- **What it is:** A guided coastal walk (Ventura backcountry or beach) where the group forages natural material along the way, then prints photograms on-site using sunlight.
- **What's included:** Guided walk, all printing materials/UV exposure setup, 2–4 finished prints per person.
- **What to bring:** Closed-toe shoes, water, a hat; optional camera for their own documentation (in keeping with "beyond the constraints of social media" — this is the un-phone version of that photo).
- **Price framing (draft only):** Position as an experience-plus-object bundle — higher than the popup given the guided/outdoor labor and duration, framed around "you'll walk away with proof you were there."

**Pitch note (for a store/brewery/museum-type owner) — draft:**
> *Hi [name] — I'm Josh Ball, a Ventura photographer and cyanotype printmaker (sponsored by Jacquard Products, founder of the Ventura Cold Water Cadre). I run hands-on popup print sessions — no screens, just sunlight and paper — where your customers make a real, one-of-a-kind piece of art in under an hour. It's a draw for foot traffic during a slow afternoon, and everyone leaves with something they made, not something they bought. I bring all materials; you bring the space. Want to pick a date?*

This mirrors the real, already-proven CIMM speaker-event format (Finding 4) but adapted for a commercial/retail host rather than a museum.
