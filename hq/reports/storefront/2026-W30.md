# Storefront Report — Week 2026-W30

Filed by: storefront agent (scheduled run)

## Findings

1. **Priority-2 revenue line (giclée prints) has zero live listings.** All five Neptune's Garden cyanotype products — the four individual coral prints plus the "Complete Collection" bundle — are in `draft` status. Per the charter, giclée prints are the accessible alternative to originals and the #2 priority revenue line; right now none of the current-generation giclée SKUs are purchasable. Source: current product catalog sync (2026-07-23).

2. **Originals (priority 1) are represented by a single design.** The only live originals are three "Lobster Season 2023" linocut listings (base print, a test print, and a suminagashi variant), priced $50–$100. The charter states "always some originals available to buy" — that's technically true, but the entire originals shelf is one subject. This is a thin buffer against the mandate once any of these one-of-a-kind pieces sells. Source: current product catalog sync.

3. **Own-art vs. Jacquard-supply inversion is more severe than the 2026-07-21 baseline figure.** Counting this sync directly: of ~107 total listings, roughly 92 (~86%) are Jacquard supply-shop SKUs, 7 are POD merch, and only 8 are own-art (of which 5 are drafts). That puts **purchasable own-art listings at 3 of 107 (~2.8%)** — this is the headline metric the directive asks me to track going forward. I flag honestly that the ~86% figure I calculate here may not be directly comparable to the "~73%" cited in the prior audit if that count used a different denominator or snapshot — I don't have that audit's raw method to reconcile against, so treat this as this week's baseline measurement rather than a claim that things got worse.

4. **Jacquard sold-out hygiene cannot be executed from this data.** Standing order 4 requires flagging sold-out Jacquard listings for restock/hide/remove, but this catalog sync only reports a binary `active`/`draft` status — no stock-level or sold-out flag per SKU. I cannot verify which (if any) of the "36+ sold out" listings referenced in the prior audit remain sold out. This is a genuine data gap, not a finding that the problem is fixed or worsened — recommend the sync be extended to include inventory counts so this standing order can actually be executed.

5. **Possible margin-floor risk on POD drinkware.** Our smallest Bodysurf Fin/Logo mug tier is priced at $9.00. Published third-party estimates of Printful's 11oz white mug base cost range widely — one source puts it at "Mugs (11 oz White Glossy Mug): Starting at $5.95", while another reports "Standard 11oz White Mug: Typically starts around $8.25 plus shipping." At the $5.95 base, our $9.00 price clears the 30% floor narrowly (~34%); at $8.25, it falls to roughly 8% — well under the floor. Our enamel cup at $14.00 also sits noticeably below a comparable third-party Printful-fulfilled enamel mug retailing at "Enamel Mug · Sale price$18.00 By Printful". I don't have Josh Ball Art's actual synced COGS, so I can't state a breach with certainty — but the spread is wide enough to warrant a real cost check before the next restock or price review.

6. **Sizing mismatch on Neptune's Garden drafts.** The directive specifies giclée sizes 8×10/11×14/16×20 with white borders; the five draft cyanotype listings also include a 5×7 size not in that standard set. Worth a quick confirm with the CEO on whether 5×7 is an intentional new tier or should be dropped for consistency before these go live.

7. **Brand/SEO naming inconsistency on 4 of 7 POD listings.** "Josh Ball Art" styling is present on the Logo Mug, the Enamel Cup, and the Tumbler titles, but missing from the Bodysurf Fin Mug, the Cuffed Beanie, and both Poster listings (plain/framed). The directive requires consistent "Josh Ball Art" SEO styling across the catalog. Draft renames proposed below (Printful name-only action, no price/brand-identity change beyond consistent styling).

8. **Etsy revival status unknown.** Per mandate, Etsy will be owned by this department "once it is revived." I ran a live handle check on `joshballart` on Etsy as forward-looking due diligence; the platform rate-limited the request (HTTP 403), so the result is inconclusive, not a real answer. This wasn't a standing-order requirement this week, but worth having on record for when Etsy revival is actually scheduled — a human should check this directly at that time.
