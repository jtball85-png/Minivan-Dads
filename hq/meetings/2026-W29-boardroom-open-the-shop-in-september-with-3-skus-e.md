## Ruling Summary

The CEO ruled that Minivan Dads will launch its shop in September with the
3 planned SKUs, rejecting the open-ended "hold until unarguably nailed"
position from creative and market_intel, and rejecting finance's implicit
"ship whatever's on hand" framing in favor of a middle path: creative runs
a short, concrete pass/fail checklist on the flagship badge (thumbnail
readability, screenshot-worthiness, no typographic/trademark issues) within
2–3 days. A pass means the launch proceeds on schedule; a fail is the only
condition under which slipping past back-to-school is justified, and any
resulting delay should be days, not an indefinite hold. The residual risk —
that the badge ships at "80%, not perfect" — is accepted explicitly, on the
grounds that POD's no-inventory model lets the design be sharpened for the
next print run based on real customer signal, at no financial cost.

Creative and market_intel dissent: both held that the badge is pillar 1 of
the business, not one SKU among three, and that a mediocre flagship risks
the one unacceptable outcome the charter names — failure to protect the
brand — making a time-boxed checklist an insufficient substitute for
getting it right before shipping. Finance and content do not dissent; their
position (launch now to capture the non-renewable back-to-school window)
was substantially adopted.

Next: creative runs the checklist this week and reports pass/fail before
the shop opens. Finance and content should proceed on the assumption the
September date holds unless creative reports a failure. No other
departments have pending action from this ruling.
