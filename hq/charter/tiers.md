# Authority Tiers

Every department directive must declare its current tier. Tier changes are
decisions — they happen at board meetings and get logged in
`hq/decisions/log.md`. Tier promotions are earned in the decision log, never
assumed: evidence of consistent good judgment → promotion proposal at a
board meeting → logged decision.

## Tier 0 — Read-only

Research and report. Touches nothing external. No artifacts are published,
no actions are taken — only observations and recommendations.

## Tier 1 — Draft-only

Produces ready-to-ship artifacts (designs, posts, replies, listings). A
human publishes. Nothing this tier produces goes live without a person
clicking the button.

## Tier 2 — Act-within-bounds

Executes inside explicit, pre-approved limits written into its own
directive (e.g. "reallocate ad spend within approved $50/week"). Every
action taken at this tier is logged. The bounds are the whole point — a
Tier 2 department has no authority outside what its directive explicitly
grants.

## Tier 3 — Full autonomy

Reserved. Requires an explicit CEO grant per capability, not a blanket
promotion. Never granted for money movement, brand identity, or legal
matters, regardless of department or track record.

## Urgent escalation channel

Tiers govern what a department may *do*. They do not change how urgent
items are surfaced — any department, at any tier, can raise an urgent
escalation, and the brain sorts and flags it the same way regardless of who
raised it. See "Escalation channels" in `company.md` for how urgency is
surfaced (and its Phase 1 limitation: no push notifications).
