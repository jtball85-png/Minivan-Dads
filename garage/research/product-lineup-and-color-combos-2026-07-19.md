# Garage Research: Product Lineup Validation + Shirt/Mug Color Combos — 2026-07-19

Requested by: CEO, as a follow-up to the hats/patches and sayings
research. Mode: multi-agent independent research (2 agents — Printful
product performance, shirt/mug color-combo preferences). Purpose:
garage-only, informs the upcoming design pass on t-shirts and mugs.
Beanies explicitly excluded from the color-combo half per the CEO's
own instruction — deferred to a separate conversation.

## 1. Bottom line

Two real findings worth acting on before drafting more designs:

1. **Stickers are a real gap in the lineup, not an afterthought.**
   Independent sources (not Printful's own marketing) consistently
   converge on stickers having the best unit economics in POD — near-
   zero return risk, strong margin, and they're a near-perfect fit for a
   saying-driven brand (cheap to test many one-liners fast).
2. **"Beanie" may be the wrong hat SKU, not just a separate topic.** The
   single most trustworthy source found — a named small-business
   owner's own review — said Printful's embroidery is genuinely
   best-in-class, but her actual bestseller was a **Classic Dad Hat
   (structured/trucker), not a beanie**, and beanies carry a winter-only
   demand ceiling a year-round brand doesn't want. Worth bringing into
   the separate beanie conversation, not just filed away.

On color: **black holds as the one universal anchor across hats, shirts,
AND mugs** — the strongest cross-product finding in all three research
passes combined. Navy and heather-grey also hold across all three.
Beyond that, each product genuinely diverges — camo is hat-only, red is
shirt-only, pink is tumbler-only, and mugs introduce a whole color axis
(light-vs-dark base, two-tone accents) that doesn't exist for apparel.

## 2. Confidence

- **High:** black/navy/heather-grey as cross-product anchors; white and
  black as the two mug base colors; Comfort Colors garment-dyed shirts
  as the dominant substrate for saying-driven dad shirts right now
  (a real difference from the hat research — no comparable single-
  substrate convergence was found there); stickers' margin/return-rate
  advantage (multiple independent sources agree); the beanie seasonality
  ceiling.
- **Moderate:** exact accent-color palettes (Printful/Printify confirmed
  the *option* for two-tone mugs exists, not which specific colors);
  red as a shirt-specific bestseller color (one brand); the beanie→dad-
  hat swap as *the* right call vs. just *a reasonable* one (one
  high-quality source, not several).
- **Explicit gap, consistent across all three research passes now:** no
  visual inspection of any product photo was possible in any pass —
  every color/style claim is inferred from listing titles and copy.
  This pass leaned on it more than the hat research did, since Etsy/
  Amazon blocked direct listing fetches almost entirely this round.

## 3. Key findings

### Product lineup (Printful performance research)

Ranked, most confident first:

1. **Stickers — add.** Best-supported margin/return-rate signal found
   across this whole research effort; near-zero fit/return risk;
   matches the brand's content directly (cheap to test many sayings).
2. **Embroidered hats — keep the category, reconsider the SKU.**
   Highest-confidence positive *quality* signal found (named-seller
   review), but margins are thin at true production cost, and beanies
   specifically have a seasonal ceiling dad hats/trucker caps don't.
3. **T-shirts — keep as the identity anchor, not the margin play.**
   Most saturated category found in this research; thinnest realistic
   margin (~12% in one detailed cost breakdown) — still the right
   product for brand recognition, just don't expect it to carry margin.
4. **Mugs — keep as a secondary/gift-attach item, not a hero product.**
   Steady demand, acceptable margin, but breakage/shipping-damage is a
   recurring cross-source cost apparel and stickers don't have.
5. **Tote bags — do not add on Printful without ordering samples
   first.** The one credible, product-matched, named-seller review found
   was explicitly negative on Printful's tote print quality.
6. **Koozies / posters — a small future test, not a lineup driver now.**
   Low-confidence, single-opinion sources only.

### Mug color combos (new territory — no prior research covered this)

Ranked, most common first:
1. White base, black text/print — the default.
2. Black base, white or multi-color text — well-established second.
3. Navy base, white text — a recurring named variant.
4. Black-inside/black-handle two-tone on a white (or colored) body —
   supply-confirmed as a standard, easy POD blank; one direct dad-humor
   example found.
5. Color-changing black→reveal heat-sensitive mugs — a real, recurring
   sub-format, not a gimmick outlier.
6. Off-white/speckled enamel + black rim — the camping-humor sub-niche's
   own distinct default.
7. Black / Navy / Pink insulated travel tumblers — pink appears *only*
   here, nowhere else across hats, shirts, or ceramic mugs.

Sizes: 11oz and 15oz ceramic are standard; enamel camp mugs and 20/30oz
tumblers are secondary formats tied to the outdoorsy/gift tier.

### Shirt color combos (extends the hat research, not a repeat of it)

Ranked, most common first:
1. Black / dark heather / charcoal, single-color (white/off-white)
   print — matches the hat research's black-anchor finding.
2. Navy — matches hats.
3. Grey/heather family — matches hats, with more named variants on
   shirts (sport grey, dark heather, heather military green, etc.).
4. Distressed/vintage print treatment — explicitly named on multiple
   Comfort Colors dad listings. **Connects directly to the patches
   research's own "distressed/vintage" typography finding** — the same
   register shows up independently on shirts too.
5. Broader blue-family spread (denim, ocean, indigo, stone blue) — real,
   but shirt-specific; nothing comparable appeared in the hat research.
6. Red — named as a bestseller color at one established brand (Dad
   Gang); not seen on hats or mugs anywhere in this research.
7. Pastel/vintage-wash (sage, lavender, melon, ivory) — real as a
   general 2026 apparel trend and as Comfort Colors' natural garment-dye
   effect, but **not confirmed as applied to dad-humor sayings
   specifically** — same caution level the hat research gave "cream as
   an accent color." Treat as a hypothesis, not a finding.

## 4. Where the two passes intersect — and one correction to the earlier hat research

**Camo is hat-specific — no mug or shirt equivalent found anywhere in
this research.** Confirms camo as a real but narrow color choice, not a
universal one.

**The minivan/"swagger wagon" white-space claim from the hats/patches
research does NOT extend to shirts.** That research found zero
minivan-specific *hat* listings. This pass found real minivan-specific
*shirt* products (a Redbubble "Swaggin Wagon MiniVan" shirt, an active
Etsy "funny minivan shirt" category) — consistent with, not
contradicting, the earlier sayings research, which already found
several minivan *shirt* slogans ("I'll Never Drive a Minivan," "Real Men
Drive Minivans," the ESC-008 Amazon shirt). **The correction is narrower
than it sounds:** minivan shirts existing was already known; what's new
here is confirming hats remain the more open lane for this specific
concept, while shirts need to win on *tone* (confident vs. the
apologetic register already found), not on being first. This wasn't
independently re-verified this pass — the agent flagged it as a gap,
not a re-confirmed finding.

## 5. What this doesn't cover

- No visual confirmation of any color claim in this pass — same
  limitation as the hat research, more pronounced here since almost
  every direct listing fetch was blocked.
- Did not verify the exact accent-color options Printful/Printify offer
  for two-tone mugs (confirmed the option exists, not the color list) —
  worth checking directly in a Printful account before finalizing a mug
  design.
- Did not re-check whether existing minivan shirts are apologetic or
  confident in tone — flagged as an open gap, not resolved here.
- Did not investigate stickers' or dad-hats' color-combo preferences —
  this pass was scoped to shirts + mugs only, per the CEO's own
  instruction to leave beanies for a separate conversation.
